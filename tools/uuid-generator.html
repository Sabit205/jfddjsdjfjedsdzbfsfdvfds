Frequently Asked Questions

What is a UUID?

A UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) is a 128-bit number used to uniquely identify information in computer systems. Because the number of possible UUIDs is so vast, the probability of two independently generated UUIDs being the same is practically zero.

What is the difference between UUID v1 and v4?

UUID v1 is generated based on a timestamp and the MAC address of the computer, making it time-ordered but potentially less private. UUID v4 is generated using random or pseudo-random numbers, making it the most common and recommended choice for ensuring uniqueness without leaking information.

Is this UUID generator secure?

Yes. This tool uses the standard 'uuid' JavaScript library to generate UUIDs entirely within your browser. No data is sent to our servers, ensuring your activity is private and secure.

Can I use these UUIDs in my projects?

Absolutely. The UUIDs generated by this tool conform to the RFC 4122 standard and are safe to use as unique identifiers in your databases, applications, and any other projects.
