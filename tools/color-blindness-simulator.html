Frequently Asked Questions

What is a color blindness simulator?

A color blindness simulator is a tool that applies a filter to an image to show how it might be perceived by a person with a specific type of color vision deficiency (CVD). It's an essential tool for designers and developers to test the accessibility of their visual content.

Why is testing for color blindness important?

Testing for color blindness helps ensure that your website, application, or design is accessible and usable by everyone. Information conveyed only by color might be lost to a person with CVD. This tool helps you identify potential issues in your designs, such as poor contrast in charts or unreadable text on colored backgrounds.

Is my image uploaded to your server?

No. For your privacy, the entire simulation process is done directly in your web browser. Your image is never uploaded to any server, ensuring your data remains completely private.
