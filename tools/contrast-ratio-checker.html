Frequently Asked Questions

What is color contrast and why is it important?

Color contrast is the difference in brightness between two colors. It's crucial for web accessibility, as it ensures that text is readable for everyone, including people with visual impairments like color blindness or low vision.

What are WCAG, AA, and AAA standards?

WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) are international standards for web accessibility. For contrast, there are two main conformance levels: Level AA (minimum) requires a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text. Level AAA (enhanced) requires a ratio of at least 7:1, providing better readability for a wider range of users.

Why does large text have different requirements?

Large text (typically defined as 18pt/24px or 14pt/18.5px bold) is easier to read, so it requires a lower contrast ratio to be accessible. For WCAG AA, large text needs a ratio of 3:1, and for AAA, it needs 4.5:1.
