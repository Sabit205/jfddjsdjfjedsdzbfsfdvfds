Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a password strong?

A strong password is long (at least 12-16 characters), complex (includes a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols), and random (doesn't use dictionary words or personal information). This combination makes it extremely difficult for automated software to guess.

Why use a random password generator?

Humans are naturally bad at creating truly random passwords. We tend to use predictable patterns. A random password generator creates passwords that have no pattern, making them much more secure against brute-force and dictionary attacks.

Is this password generator secure?

Yes, completely. This tool generates passwords directly in your browser using JavaScript. The generated passwords are never sent over the internet or stored on our servers, ensuring only you can see them.

How does the password strength indicator work?

The strength indicator provides a visual estimate of the password's security. It's based on two main factors: the length of the password and the variety of character types used (uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols). Longer passwords with more character types are considered stronger and will show a better score.
